CANNAINSIDER NEWS · 23 JULY 2026 · FRANZ KAFKA TODAY · REPORT 23072026-004

Ministry: We cannot assist. The jurisdictional ping-pong is heading to court

On 23 July 2026, Dušan Dvořák filed an intervention action against the Ministry of the Interior with the Prague Municipal Court. It challenges how communication MV-114818-2/TP-2026 of 21 July 2026 jointly disposed of two substantively different complaints of 15 and 17 July.

9/9EXTREME RELEVANCE · ON FIRE

Four sentences versus nineteen pages: The Ministry stated that it could not assist the claimant and that the matters described did not fall within its competence. The action alleges that the reply did not distinguish the individual parts, state the outcome of their examination, or explain whether and where any part was forwarded.

What the claimant seeks: The Ministry should assess each filing by its actual content and clearly state which parts it examined, handles under another procedure, forwarded, or specifically considers outside its competence.

What the action does not seek: It does not ask the administrative court to determine the correct THC measurement method, review criminal judgments, declare innocence, or dictate the outcome of ministerial oversight.

Evidence boundary: Filing the action does not itself prove an unlawful intervention. The Prague Municipal Court will decide admissibility, evidence and outcome. The new proceeding has not yet received a case number.

Procedural web: The action identifies three other active intervention cases: 18 A 17/2026, 18 A 23/2026 and 8 Ad 9/2026. Their number alone proves no illegality; it shows how related questions were divided among several authorities and judicial proceedings.

Full source and evidence boundary

Mgr. Dušan Dvořák, action for protection against unlawful intervention by the Ministry of the Interior, filed by data box on 23 July 2026; challenged communication MV-114818-2/TP-2026.
