Someone cannot count. Three cannabis plants are not six or seven

A joint complaint by G. F. and J. K. and its supplement show how criminal proceedings can produce a dispute over even an elementary plant count.

7/9HIGH PROCEDURAL RELEVANCE
Ostrava District Courtcase 15 T 11/2025Anonymised sources

The joint complaint by G. F. and J. K. dated 22 July 2026 and its supplement dated 23 July 2026, in the matter before the Ostrava District Court under case reference 15 T 11/2025, document the kinds of mathematical constructs that may arise in criminal proceedings.

The complaint states that the two complainants jointly grew a total of three growing plants, alongside one drying plant, and that one of the three growing plants was mouldy. It also alleges that state authorities worked with a claim of six growing plants and one dried plant.

Three jointly grown plants do not mean three for each person. Higher cannabis mathematics, indeed.

The complainant is vice-chair of the governing board of Edukativní konopná klinika, z. s. Personal details of both complainants were removed from the public copies.

What is documented

The exact dates, court, case reference, the complainants' account of the number of jointly grown plants, withdrawal of the previous text while retaining already submitted evidentiary attachments, and the content of the new joint filing.

What this report does not claim

Publication and the traffic-light score do not predict the court's decision. The competent court will assess the procedural effect of the withdrawal and new filing.
